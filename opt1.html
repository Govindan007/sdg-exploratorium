Climate Change Basics

Understanding Climate Change

Climate change refers to significant changes in global temperatures and weather patterns over time. While climate change is a natural phenomenon, scientific evidence shows that human activities, particularly the burning of fossil fuels, are driving an unprecedented rate of change.

What Causes Climate Change?

  • Greenhouse Gas Emissions: The burning of fossil fuels like coal, oil, and gas releases carbon dioxide (CO2) and other greenhouse gases into the atmosphere, trapping heat.
  • Deforestation: Trees absorb CO2; when they are cut down, not only is this CO2 not absorbed, but the carbon stored in trees is released back into the atmosphere.
  • Agriculture: Livestock and certain farming practices release methane, another potent greenhouse gas.

Effects of Climate Change

Climate change has far-reaching impacts, including:

  1. Rising Sea Levels: Melting ice caps and glaciers contribute to rising sea levels, threatening coastal communities.
  2. Extreme Weather: Increased frequency and intensity of hurricanes, droughts, and floods disrupt ecosystems and human lives.
  3. Impacts on Biodiversity: Many species struggle to adapt to changing climates, leading to habitat loss and extinction.
